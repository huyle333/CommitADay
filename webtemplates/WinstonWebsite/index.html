Music in Motion

Music In Motion is Leap Motion powered virtual turntable. The turn table is controlled by simply hand motions. Fast forward and rewind by moving your hand back and forth respectively and moving your hand left or right muted the respecitive audio track are the current features impletmented.

There is also a an equilizer component that uses RGB LEDs. The equalizer simply reads changes in voltage via a transistor from an audio source and controls the voltage output to the LED strips.

This was a quick weekend project to help promote my hackerspace at Boston University's Splash. Splash is a an event for incoming or returning students to find clubs that they're interest in.

Canary

BattleHack-Boston: Second Place

Canary is a crowd source solution to mapping pollution levels. Canary uses humidity, temperature, sound, carbon monoxide and methane sensors that are connected to an Arduino Uno to measure the various pollution in any given location.

My team also used a bluetooth low energy module to connect to an android device to transmit the data from the sensors. The Android app would mark the current location via GPS and send the data to our backend.We also had a website available to display the data from our backend in a visually appealing way on Google Maps. We also provided local orginizations where people can donate to help fight pollution in their areas.

RPI Light Tricks

I built a light pulse reader during MassHack as a one way communication from an iPhone to a Raspberry Pi. The Raspberry Pi would then communicate with our parse backend.This is just a smaller complement to my friends' TimeConnect project.

How it works: I built a simple circuit with a cheap photoresistor module that was connected to a Raspberry Pi. On the Raspberry Pi, I wrote several python scripts to handle data coming from the photoresistor, to detect and convert the data into a custom binary library (for quick reads) which is converted to morse code to generate a sting of characters (serves as a unique id) to be sent to a Parse database.

Echo Can

In honor of the Boston Marathon Bombing Victims, Intrepid Labs held Hack Boston Strong with the perspective of giving back to Boston. My team: Huy Le, Dean Shi and Corey Prak decided to build a hybrid recycling and trash can, that uses sound to determine whether an item should be recycled or trashed.

At the last minute, I decided to help my friends with this hack. My team came unprepared, with no hardware tools that I needed to effieciently accomplish what we visualized, so I improvised. I came up with a transistor amplifier circuit, with the help of Dean Shi, to reverse a speaker to a microphone. We connected this cicuit to an Arduino Uno to measure the frequency through a python library.

SnappyLink

Intuit's Best Application to Simplify the Business Life

Snappy Link is a chrome extension that opens up links in an iframe by hovering over that link. This allows the user to quickly view web pages without having to click on the link and switching to the new tab. Also, this extension can continually open links within an iframe, which essentially lowers the amount of tabs opened in the user's web browser. As a plus, this works on most images and videos.

Zumba Fitness's Node Defender

First and Second Place

At the Ultimate Developer's Event, Zumba Fitness created a game, where the player would write javascript to achieve the highest possbile score in a survival game.
